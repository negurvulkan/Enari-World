# Gender modifiers and lines

The cluster system applies species-wide. Enu, Enel, Enor, Enath and Enis do not have their own cluster worlds, but rather the same basic profiles with different readability.

## How gender markers modulate

| Gender | typical modulation |
|---|---|
| Enu | softer transitions, more diffuse patterns, more visible glandular zones and surface shine |
| Enel | high readability on the face and neck; Iris, eye shape, masks and fine lines stand out |
| Enor | more robust texture, greater contrasts, harder pattern effect, proportions dominate |
| Enath | precision instead of surface contrast; Iris order, pattern fineness and head shape geometry become important |
| Enis | standardized lines, more homogeneous pigment, highly ordered iris; Cartilage ridges and protective plate structure define the line with |

The genders do not change which cluster is possible, but rather which marker field is read first.

## Enu

With Enu, many clusters appear softer and more flowing. Differences in undertone remain visible, but edges, lines and contrast masks often appear less harsh. At the same time, zones rich in glands can give a more satin impression, which in everyday life often leads to false conclusions about “mildness”.

## Enel

Enel are particularly readable at a dialogue distance. The face, neck and eye area have an above-average number of usable markers. That's why clusters at Enel are often recognized more quickly, but also overinterpreted more quickly.

## Enor

Enor is dominated by more robust surfaces, clearer contrasts and a stronger impression of the proportion signature. This can make the same clusters appear “harder” than with Enu or Enel. In particular, C5, C8 and C10 are often perceived to be very present in high levels.

## Enath

Enath often show clusters in a fine, technical-looking form. Instead of large surfaces, order, line precision and head shape geometry catch the eye. Institutionally, Enath often has fewer official lines because the number of cases is lower and the administration prefers collection categories that are as robust as possible.

## Enis

Enis are standardized into five official lines in many archives. This follows less biology than the Enarian ordering principle of five and the central supervision of a rare gender. Biologically, more variants can also occur in Enis; Officially, however, they are often contracted together.

## Management effect: line instead of full variance

The rarer a gender is, the more administration intervenes. This creates “lines”, i.e. institutionally shortened cluster families. This means:

- The biology is finer than the files.
- Rare genders officially appear more homogeneous than they actually are.
- Head shape, cartilage structure and iris order are given greater weight than broad area markers in Enath and Enis.

This administrative effect is an important world-building point because it shows how much enarian order systems shape perception.
