# Enor

![](icon:gender/enor_gender "icon-inline|width=1.25rem") The Enor are one of the five Enari genders and make up around 19% of the population. Biologically, they are specialized in physical performance and the provision of cell-containing reproductive substances.

Within Enari reproduction, Enor make a crucial contribution to embryonic development, as their secretions contain living cells and genetic components that are integrated into the brood matrix. Socially, they are often considered the physically strongest gender and therefore often take on tasks in which strength, resilience and protective functions are central.

![[../../../99_Medien/03_Fotos_und_Portraits/03_Enor/enor__foto__front__foto__v02.png|caption=Frontal reference view of an Enor.|medium|right|popover]]

## Core properties

- about 19% of the population
- largest and strongest physique of the five sexes
- high maximum strength, explosive movements and strong resilience
- reproductive contribution through cellular and genetic components
- Robust constitution with strong skeletal, muscular and circulatory systems
- common roles in military, security, heavy labor and infrastructure

## Social role

In many Enarian societies, Enor form the backbone of military and security structures. Their physical presence, their stability and their ability to function under high stress make them a central component of protection and order systems.

Even outside of these areas, Enor often work in fields that require high levels of physical resilience, such as construction, transport, mining or other forms of large-scale infrastructure work.

## Psychology and behavior

The psychological character of the Enor is associated in many cultures with determination, steadfastness and loyalty. They are often seen as direct, pragmatic and action-oriented.

At the same time, their social protective and work roles often lead to cultural expectations that particularly emphasize strength, reliability and assertiveness. Many Enor develop a strong sense of duty towards their group, unit or task.
