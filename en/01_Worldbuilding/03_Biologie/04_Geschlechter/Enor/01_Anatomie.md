# Anatomy of Enor

## General appearance

On average, Enor have the largest and strongest physique of the five genders. She is tall in stature, with broad shoulders, a strong chest and well-developed muscles. This constitution gives them high maximum strength and excellent resilience under difficult physical conditions.

The Enor's organism is designed to cope with both high mechanical stress and to produce large amounts of cell-containing reproductive secretions. Accordingly, your body combines a robust supporting structure with a powerful tissue supply.

## Body structure and skeletal system

The Enor's skeleton is particularly solid and stable. The bones are dense and resilient, especially in the spine, pelvis and long bones of the limbs.

The shoulder girdle is wide and allows for strong muscular anchoring of the arms. The rib structure forms a large and stable ribcage that supports both breathing and the supply of an efficient circulatory system.

The pelvis is strongly built and provides space for the Enor's reproductive gland complexes. At the same time, it contributes significantly to stabilizing the body during heavy physical stress.

## Musculature and strength profile

The Enor's muscles are particularly well developed and designed for maximum strength and explosive movements. Large muscle groups in the back, shoulders and thighs enable powerful movements and the ability to carry heavy loads.

Compared to other genders, Enor have a very high proportion of fast-twitch muscle fibers. This gives them a high acceleration ability and a pronounced physical presence.

In addition to the external muscles, Enor also have specialized smooth muscles in the reproductive organs. This enables the controlled release of cell-containing secretions during reproductive processes.

## Cardiovascular system

The Enor's cardiovascular system is designed to supply large muscle masses. Your heart is particularly powerful in relation to your body size and can move large volumes of blood per heartbeat.

A dense vascular network supplies the large muscle groups as well as the reproductive glands with oxygen and nutrients. This adaptation supports both intensive physical work and the energy-intensive production of reproductive cell components.

During physical exertion, Enor's circulatory system can respond quickly to increased demands and maintain a stable oxygen supply.

## Breathing and metabolism

The Enor's lung capacity is increased compared to most other genders. A large chest enables efficient breathing and supports the oxygen supply to the strong muscles.

The Enor's metabolism is correspondingly efficient. They can convert large amounts of nutrients into mechanical work or reproductive cell production. This often results in a higher energy requirement than other genders.

## Skin and sensory organs

The Enor's skin is relatively thick and resilient. It protects the body from mechanical stress and minor injuries. The subcutaneous tissue is usually less pronounced than in Enu, which means the muscles are more clearly visible.

Their sensory organs largely correspond to the general Enarian pattern. However, the sense of balance and spatial perception, which are of great importance for coordinated, powerful movement, are particularly well developed.

## Endocrine system

The Enor's endocrine system supports both their physical performance and the production of reproductive cell components. Hormonal circuits control muscle growth, energy balance and the activity of the reproductive glands.

During reproductive activity, hormonal production can increase sharply. This activation phase promotes the formation of cell-containing secretions, which are later introduced into the breeding matrix.## Sexual characteristics and reproductive organs

The primary sexual characteristics of the Enor consist of a specialized gland complex in the ventral pelvic region. This complex produces a cell-containing secretion that is introduced into the brood matrix during reproduction.

The secretion contains genetic material as well as living cells necessary for the embryonic development of the Enari. The release takes place via several ducts and is controlled by muscular contractions.

Externally, this complex appears as a *campus cellularis* with six *ostia vehicularia* and a central *meatus emissorius*. The structure, ripening chambers and delivery logic are described in detail in [03_Fortpflanzungsorgane.md](./03_Fortpflanzungsorgane.md).
