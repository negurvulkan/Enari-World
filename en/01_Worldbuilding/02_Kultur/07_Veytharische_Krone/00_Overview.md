## Short profile

The Veythar Crown is a traditional state system in which an Enis serves as the personified head of the empire. In High Veythar, the form of government can be referred to as `Veythar-naar vezeth-joreth`, i.e. the Veythar heartland of the crown order. The ruler is not determined by descent in the human sense, but is chosen from a small, strictly filtered circle of suitable Enis. The Crown or `vezeth` is not a private property, but a lifelong or very long office with real power, ritualized dignity and clearly defined state functions.

The monarchy thus remains monarchical, enarian and functional at the same time. She has a court center, a single crown and a high visibility tip without relying on father-mother-child lines. At the same time, it is not a city state, but rather a monarchical territorial state and great power association with heartland, core provinces, brands, threshold areas and imperially influenced outer areas. Their idea of ​​legitimation is not simply that Enis are rare, but that the five-member society of the empire can only be visibly bundled in a properly organized leadership.

Conceptually, the system consciously distinguishes between `vezar` as the current ruler, `vezeth` as an office, `vezeth-yemeth` as the seat of the throne or office, `vezeth-joreth` as the entire crown order and the crown empire as the concrete territorial state that this order supports.

## Canon in short form

- The Veythar Crown is a caste- and office-based monarchy with a single crown bearer at the top.
- Only Enis from an exclusive, institutionally tested circle of suitability are eligible for crown recognition.
- Succession is not dynastically hereditary, but is tied to selection, confirmation and ritual elevation.
- The `vezeth` embodies supreme arbitration, final decision on constitutional, rank and military issues as well as the appointment of central officials.
- Day-to-day administration lies with `wereth-drun` chanceries, `nivar` and provincial bodies, `gareth` and ritual institutions and not with a private ruling family.
- The empire of the Veythar Crown is not just a city state, but a hierarchical large and territorial state with graduated territorial integration.
- Not all spaces are equally integrated: heartland, core provinces, brands, threshold spaces and outdoor spaces follow different levels of connection.
- The Crown names its large areas with its own room types such as `vezeth-ireth`, `koren-naar`, `selar-naar`, `mureth-naar` and `moran-naar` instead of with earthly provincial or noble titles.
- In crises, the direct power of the crown increases significantly without the state becoming a mere military dictatorship.
- Ideologically, the Crown sees itself as a concentrated voice of order and thus as a conscious counterpoint to the broader participatory Veyrath Republic.
