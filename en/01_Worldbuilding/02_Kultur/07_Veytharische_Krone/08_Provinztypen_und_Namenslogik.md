# Veytharian Crown - Province types and naming logic

## Target

This chapter determines how the Veytharian Crown officially names its large areas and how a first imperial map makes these areas legible. Unlike many terrestrial monarchies, the crown does not work with counties or duchies, but with veyatic spatial heads in which landscape, function and degree of connection coincide.

## Basic principle

- The Crown prefers to use `-naar` as the territorial head for large areas of the empire. A space is therefore not just an administrative unit, but a bound land or area of ​​origin.
- The actual place or region name is in front of it; the official room class can be added to the full title.
- This makes even political terms seem like landscape names. This fits with the crown idea that rule orders spaces instead of just counting districts.

## Official room types

| German reading | Veythar type | Function | political reading |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Crown Heartland | `vezeth-ireth` | innermost courtyard, throne and archive room | narrowest core of the crown, not a normal province |
| Heartland / Core Province | `koren-naar` | fully integrated main room | Generic term for firmly integrated imperial landscapes |
| Coastal Core Kingdom | `verun-naar` | maritime core province | Port, customs, fleet, diplomatic coast |
| Valley or Ritual Core Realm | `doren-naar` | internal and ritual-centered core province | Traditional spaces, old school and elevation valleys |
| Brand Country | `selar-naar` | Border and protection province | military stamp |
| Emerging country | `mureth-naar` | Transitional and mixed province | Transit, special supervision, sensitive loyalty situation |
| External space / orbital territory | `moran-naar` | orbital or extraplanetary crown space | imperial periphery, spaceports, corridors, resource zones |
| Lunar Territory | `saren-naar` | moonbound crown space | Subform of the `moran-naar`, mostly military-logistical |

## Internal grading of the core provinces

Not every core province is built the same. `koren-naar` is the generic term for the most solid areas of the empire; The crown primarily distinguishes between these:

- `verun-naar` for seaside and commercial core landscapes
- `doren-naar` for deeper, ritual-dense and internally oriented core spaces

Other sub-forms can be added later, such as field or forest-related core landscapes. The only important thing is that the Crown does not first read its rooms based on ethnicity, but rather based on location, ties and function.

## Name patterns for cards and registers

The naming follows the Vatican legal system: the last term determines the location type.

| Pattern | Example | typical reading |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `[Bestimmer] + verun` | `Savir-verun` | Port or coastal area |
| `[Bestimmer] + doren` | `Var-doren` | Valley, inland or ritual space |
| `[Bestimmer] + selar` | `Draes-selar` | hard border hem, brand space |
| `[Amtswort] + ireth` | `vezeth-ireth` | Hof- und Herzlandkern |
| `[Regionalname] + [Raumklasse]` | `Savir-verun verun-naar` | Full title of a province in maps, decrees and registers |

In everyday life and diplomacy, the short name is often sufficient. In official maps, appointments and `wereth-drun` registers, however, the full spatial class appears preferentially.

## Reading logic of a first empire map

A usable first empire map of the crown should distinguish at least these levels:

1. `vezeth-ireth` as a heartland around the throne seat, court axes and central archives
2. `koren-naar` as the upper ring of the closest core provinces
3. `verun-naar` and `doren-naar` as the most important subtypes of the core landscapes
4. `selar-naar` as a brand arch and mountain border
5. `mureth-naar` as politically sensitive transition zones
6. `moran-naar` as orbital and extraplanetary outer spaces
7. `saren-naar` as special lunar territories with their own symbol or hatch code

This makes the map legible not just geographically, but constitutionally.

## Political sense of the guys

The room types reveal a lot about the ideology of the crown:- The Heartland is not simply the capital area, but the visible inner core of the order.
- Core provinces are read as supporting areas of the empire, not as interchangeable districts.
- Brands and threshold spaces are not mere edges, but test points of the crown power.
- External spaces make the imperial reach visible without the crown having to adopt a foreign imperial vocabulary.

This is precisely why the Veythar Crown remains linguistically and politically independent. It seems like an empire that names its territories based on its own world and spatial logic.
