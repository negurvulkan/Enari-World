# Veythar Crown - symbolism and insignia

## State emblem of the crown

The symbolic language of the Veythar Crown is not open and republican, but rather condensing, axial and emphasizing rank. Its most important signs show that `joreth` should reach a peak without ending in mere private luxury.

| characters | shape | statement |
| ------- | ---- | ------- |
| Crown of office or `vezeth` | metal circlet with five ascending struts that gather into a narrow point above the head | the five-part society is bound together in a single form of sovereignty |
| `vezeth-yemeth` | heavy seat made of stone, metal and dark wood with a clear central axis | the institution is more permanent than the individual |
| Crown Seal | condensed national emblem with central axis and surrounding ring | final decision, unity and undistributed tip |
| Official colors | Salt White, Deep Bronze, Sea Green and Night Black | Coastal space, metal hardness, rank dignity and controlled seriousness |
| Guiding formula | `Nicht viele Stimmen tragen das Reich, sondern die richtig geordnete Stimme an seiner Spitze.` | philosophical justification of concentrated authority |

## Insignia and their logic

The crown insignia belongs to the `vezeth`, not the respective `vezar`. Typical insignia are:

- the official crown as a visible symbol of the concentrated leadership
- a sealing staff or judgment staff for arbitration, appointment and final decision
- the large state seal for `vezeth-kevar`, treaties and imperial decrees
- a specific type of cloak or shawl that the private person takes back under official form

After death, deposition or deposition, these characters return to the `vezeth-yemeth`. This is precisely why the crown remains institutional and not family dynastic.

## Festivals and ritual annual rhythm

The Veytharian Crown has a festive calendar that is less focused on popular participation than on the renewal of rank, loyalty and orderly visibility.

| day | Content | Atmosphere |
| --- | ------ | ----------- |
| `Tag der Erhebung` | commemorating the installation of the current or canonical first crown; `vezeth-kevar` oaths renewed, insignia publicly displayed | solemn, strict, strongly court-centered |
| `Tag der stillen Schlichtung` | Reading major `thereth` verdicts, pardons and symbolic reclassifications | calm, tense, characterized by legal dignity |
| `Grenzschwur` | Guards, marquee guards and governors renew their ties of loyalty to the crown | martial, disciplined, strongly influenced by Enor |
| `Nacht der Hallen` | Archives, ritual houses and ancient throne sites are opened; the continuity of the empire is demonstrated | dark, reverent, steeped in tradition |

## Visibility in the empire

The symbolism of the crown does not only appear near the palace. She shapes:

- Official halls and provincial seats
- Troop banners and rank insignia
- `thereth-drun` courtrooms and `soveth-drun` audience houses
- Training locations for court service and administration

As a result, even distant provinces encounter less an abstract state brand than a dense image order based on rank and center.
