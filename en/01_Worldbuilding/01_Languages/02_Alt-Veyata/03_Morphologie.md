# Old Veyata - Morphology

## Morphological profile
- Old Veyata is visibly younger than Ur-Veyatic, but even more formal than Veyrathi.
- Roots remain clearly visible; Functional marking is distributed among suffixes, clitics and free particles.
- Compared to the daughter languages, Old Veyata is the actual hinge level: old forms are still there, new simplifications are already taking effect.

## Personal pronouns

Like Ur-Veyata, Old Veyata maintains a double system of longer and shorter forms. The shorter forms are normal in current speech; the longer forms carry height, distance, ritual or contrast.

| Function | Short form | full form | Oblique | Genitive/possessive |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Enu | `nu` | `nau` | `nui` | `nu=ren` |
| Enel | `nel` | `nela` | `neli` | `nel=ren` |
| Enor | `nor` | `nora` | `nori` | `nor=ren` |
| Enath | `tha` | `thaya` | `thai` | `tha=ren` |
| Enis | `is` | `isha` | `isi` | `is=ren` |
| neutral/foreign polite | `se` | `seya` | `sei` | `se=ren` |
| impersonal | `ve` | `veya` | `vei` | `ve=ren` |

### Reflexive
- `ser` = yourself
- Example: `Nor seyra ser.` = The Enor sees himself.

### Pronominal plural
- Animate or group plurals are often formed with `-iri`.
- Shortened forms are already beginning to circulate in ongoing speech.

Examples:
- `nu` -> `nun` / `nuiri`
- `nel` -> `neliri`
- `nor` -> `noriri`
- `tha` -> `thairi`
- `is` -> `isiri`
- `se` -> `sen`

Note:
- This is precisely where the strong drift sets in later: Veyrathi standardizes shorter series, Veytharisch builds up new oblique and possessive series.

## Nominal morphology

### Relations

Old Veyata no longer has a full case series, but has three stable relations:

| Relation | Markers | Core function |
|---|---|---|
| Direct | unmarked | Subject, neutral object, predicate noun |
| Oblique | `-i` | Target, receiver, marked object, older locative remains |
| Genitive/possessive | `=ren` | Possession, belonging, origin, institutional connection |

Examples:
- `thalor` = Advice
- `thalori` = the council/concerning the council
- `thalor=ren` = of the Council

### Number

| Number | Markers | Use |
|---|---|---|
| singular | Basic shape | unmarked |
| animated/social plural | `-iri` | People, groups, personal collectives |
| collective/neutral plural | `-na` | Things, substances, quantities, collective names |

Examples:
- `seror` -> `seroriri`
- `drun` -> `drunna`
- `naar` -> `naarna`
- `enar` -> `enariri`

## Determiner

| shape | Function |
|---|---|
| `ara` | determined, close, known together |
| `ena` | indefinite |
| `ora` | distant, discursively distant, otherworldly |

- `ara` marks even more than just definition; it signals a divided horizon.
- `ora` is significantly more deictic than the later Veyrathian `or`.

## Possession
- Possession usually appears as a genitive/possessive clitic before the noun:
  - `nu=ren drun`
  - `se=ren thalor`
  - `nor=ren kevara`
- `=ren` marks not only ownership, but also origin, jurisdiction and affiliation.

## Adjectives
- Basic position: after the noun
  - `ara thalor zeya`
  - `ena kevara zorin`
- Prefix is solemn, contrastive or poetic
  - `draz ara thalor`
  - `zeya ora veyratha`
- In oblique noun phrases, the adjective can be marked with `-i`:
  - `ara thalori zeyai`

### degree particles
| shape | Function |
|---|---|
| `isa` | reinforced |
| `lena` | weakens |

Examples:
- `ara nor isa draz`
- `ara nor lena draz`

## Verbs

### Basic shape
- The lexicon standard normally ends with `-a`.
- This form is at the same time the simple present form of reality.

Examples:
- `veyra` = speak
- `seyra` = see
- `tala` = go
- `nava` = give
- `wera` = write

### Tense and aspect| Category | Education | Example | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| present tense | Basic shape | `Nu veyra.` | I speak. |
| past | `-na` | `Nu veyrana.` | I spoke. |
| Future/Intentional | Verb + `ra` | `Nu veyra ra.` | I will speak. |
| History | `sai` + verb | `Nu sai veyra.` | I'm talking. |
| Resultative | `hana` + mostly past tense | `Nu hana veyrana.` | I have spoken. |
| Irrealis | `kei` + verb | `Nu kei veyra.` | I would speak. |

### Negation
- Basic negator: `no`
- Neutral pattern: `Subjekt + no + (sai/hana/kei) + Verb + (ra)`

Examples:
- `Nu no veyra.`
- `Nu no sai veyra.`
- `Nu no veyrana.`

### Imperative
- Singular and direct address: naked verb form
  - `Veyra!`
  - `Tala!`
- Prohibitive: `no` + imperative
  - `No veyra!`
  - `No tala!`

### Non-verbal predication
- In the unmarked present, Old Veyata, like later Veyrathi, can remain copulaless:
  - `ara thalor zeya`
  - `nu=ren drun torim`
- For marked tense, negation or unreal contexts, `fera` serves as a supporting verb:
  - `ara toren no fera torim`
  - `ara thalor ferana zeya`
  - `ara toren kei fera torim`

## Word formation

### Productive endings
| Function | Markers | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Event / abstract | `-atha` | `veyratha` |
| agent | `-or` | `veyror` |
| relational adjective | `-i` | `kevari` |

### Prefixes
- `ba-` targeted
- `eth-` withdrawing
- `vae-` forming
- `kir-` separating
- `ret-` backwards
- `il-`
- `uth-`
- `syn-` together

Examples:
- `rettala`
- `synzora`
- `vaekova`
