# Saithan - Examples

## Notes on the register
- The following sentences are in a formal, didactic register.
- Basic pattern of prose: mostly `Subjekt - Objekt - Verb`.
- Genitives come before the head word; Cases are marked with suffixes.
- The copula `esk` is very common in definitions, legal formulas and doctrines.

## Basic sets
- `Saith thaelaith esk.` = Knowledge is founded on principle.
- `Skaivor morkein skaivek.` = The taxonomist classifies the substance class.
- `Kaivor kaivethis morketh kaivek.` = The measurer determines the substance by measurement.
- `Mnoror mnorvaurur mnorum mnorek.` = The archivist records the entire corpus in the archive house.
- `Kethor kethumen iskareth mnorek.` = The lawyer records the definition of the code.
- `Thaelor saithar selthath.` = The teacher should order based on knowledge.
- `Morketh morkeinath skaivek.` = Substance is classified as a substance class.
- `Ul iskareth, norvain na esk.` = Without definition there is no valid derivation.
- `Draukor kethselthum drauken.` = The auditor checked the procedural rules.
- `Saithen mnoror saithvaurur esen.` = The knowledge archivist was in the teaching house.

## Interlinear glosses
Legend: `GNOM` = gnomic teaching form, `DOC` = documentary past, `INJ` = injunctive/ritual form, `GEN` = genitive, `DAT` = dative/directive, `LOC` = locative, `INS` = Instrumental, `ESS` = essive/classificative, `NEG` = negation, `PRIV` = privative.

## Definitions and tenets
- `Saith draukethis esk.` = Knowledge comes from proof.
- `Keth norvainis esk.` = Law applies through consistency.
- `Mnorum ul mnoreth na esk.` = An archive corpus without recording is not valid.
- `Skaiveth ul thael na esk.` = Classification without principle does not apply.
- `Selthum kethin esk.` = The rules of procedure are legally binding.
- `Eirneth draukethar norvek.` = Observation leads to proof.

## Ritual and legal formulas
- `Kethum esath.` = The code applies.
- `Mnoris draukketh esk.` = Through the register the precedent applies.
- `Saithar mnor, kethar selth.` = For knowledge the entry, for law the procedure.
- `Ul keth, vaur na esk.` = No institution exists without law.

## Comment style
- `Thaelor saithaith mnorek; saithor norvain norvek.` = The preceptor records the teaching, the scholar derives the conclusion.
- `Eirnor eirneth mnorek; draukor drauketh selthek.` = The observer records the findings, the examiner organizes the evidence.
- `Kethor iskarketh mnorek; iskaror kethaith norvek.` = The lawyer records the definition norm, the definer interprets the legal doctrine.
