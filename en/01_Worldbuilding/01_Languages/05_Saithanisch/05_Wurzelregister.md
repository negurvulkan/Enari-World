# Saithanic - root register

## Purpose

This register controls the major expansion of Saithan into scientific, archive and technical language. It is not a replacement for `03_Lexikon.md`, but the productive control document for new terms in physics, chemistry, biology and related disciplines.

This starting version contains `71` scientifically productive roots. `9` explicit forms are noted per root:
- the bare root
- three leading forms
- five forms of technology

Calculation basis:
- `71` roots x `9` forms = `639` explicitly noted specialist forms
- together with the `79` core lexemes from `03_Lexikon.md` = at least `718` documented Saithan forms

## Status key

- `Kern` = central root of the Saithan scholarly language
- `System` = preferred root for methodological or technical training
- `Fach` = strongly domain-bound root for a specific scientific field

## Work rules

- New technical terms should first be formed from existing roots and standard suffixes.
- Before a new root is created, it must be checked whether `-eth`, `-aith`, `-or`, `-in`, `-um`, `-akh`, `-ir` or `-ein` fit an existing root.
- Particularly preferred are two-part composites made of `Fachwurzel + Methodwurzel`, such as `grathkaiveth`, `solkdraukakh` or `kelneirnakh`.
- Heavier clusters may be retained in scientific registries; Saithanic smooths specialized forms significantly less than the Veyatic languages.
