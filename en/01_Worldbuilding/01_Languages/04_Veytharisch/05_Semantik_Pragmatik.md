# Veytharisch - semantics and pragmatics

## Social function
- Veytharian is less neutral than Veyrathi and carries stronger signals of affiliation.
- Those who speak Veythar often mark origins, ties, loyalty or situational proximity.
- In trade and administration, people often switch between Veyrathi and Veythar: facts and standards in Veyrathi, attitude and commitment in Veythar.

## Pronoun choice
- `sae` remains the safe neutral form for strangers, unclear situations and official speech.
- More specific person forms such as `nor`, `thae` or `ish` appear closer, more precise and more socially integrated.
- The change from `sae` to a specific form can already be read in dialogues as a relationship signal.

## Item usage
- The definite article `ae` is more common than in Veyrathi and often marks not only definitiveness but shared situational knowledge.
- Zero articles occur, but appear more concise, poetic or protocol-like.
- In legal language, `ae` strongly binds speakers to the discourse and is deliberately used accordingly.

## Ownership and belonging
- The `na` construction encodes not only ownership, but also origin, responsibility and institutional ties:
  - `ae thal na saem` = the person's advice / advice assigned to the person
  - `ae verun na talen` = the city's port
- Attributive possessives without `na` sound denser and more formal:
  - `nain drun`
  - `saen kevar`

## Typical discourse particles
| shape | Function | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| `hae` | soft salutation, introduction | opens the train carefully |
| `vel` | Agreement, alignment | good, understood, agreed |
| `drai` | Correction, counteraction | contradicts or redirects |
| `shem` | Hesitation, search signal | holds the right to speak |
| `ouh` | Astonishment, warning | marks surprise or danger |

Examples:
- `Hae, vel.` = Yes, I'm listening.
- `Drai, ae kevar ne is zorin.` = No, the contract is incorrect.
- `Shem, nai meir...` = Uh, I know...

## Stylistic tendencies
- Main sentences with a full V2 pattern appear close to standard and controlled.
- Elimination of articles, greater sound reduction and shorter verb forms have a coastal and everyday effect.
- In oaths, oaths and council speeches, old cognates and conservative compounds are intentionally preserved to signal tradition.

## Overall impression
- Veytharisch seems more personal, dense and socially marked than Veyrathi.
- Where Veyrathi sounds transparent and learning-friendly, Veytharisch sounds more binding, local and emotionally closer.
