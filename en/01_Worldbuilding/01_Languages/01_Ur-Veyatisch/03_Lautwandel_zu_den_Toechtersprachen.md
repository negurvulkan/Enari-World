# Ur-Veyatic - sound change to the daughter languages

## Basic idea
Ur-Veyatic is not built as a mere hybrid form, but as a common precursor with its own form. The two daughter languages have generalized different sides of it:

- Veyrathi smoothes, opens and regularly.
- Veytharisch condenses, shortens and preserves edges.

## Regular developments
| Ur-Veyatic | Veyrathi | Veythar | Typical effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| `ey` | `ey` | `ei` | Diphthong stays vs. is pulled forward |
| `aer` | `ar` | `ae` | deictic article is narrowed in different ways |
| `aur` | `or` | `our` | Distal form splits in two directions |
| Verb on `-ar` | Lexicon compliant on `-a` | Lexicon conforms to `-en`, finite root form | both daughter languages ​​build new verb paradigms |
| Abstract `-at` | `-ath` | `-eth` | The end of the word is ceremoniously crossed out vs. lifted and moved |
| Animatesplural `-i` | `-ir` | `-ei` | social majority remains visible, but developed differently |
| weak secondary vowels | often received | often reduced | Veyrathi opens, Veytharically condensed |
| limited initial clusters | further simplified | partially preserved | e.g. E.g. `shr`, `thr`, `khl` |
| free adjective position with suffix as a basis | Reenactment becomes normal | Prefixing becomes normal | same source, different generalization |
| optional copula `isar` | present mostly lost, later replaced by `fera` | expanded as `is/isen` | different solution for marked predication |
| mixed sentence order | SVO becomes standard | V2/main clause + verb-late subordinate clauses | Pragmatic old variation is standardized differently |

## Important irregularities
- High-frequency pronouns drift apart early; `nau` in particular develops irregular short forms.
- Some ritual words retain more conservative residues in Veythar, while Veyrathi rearranges them semantically.
- Not every Veythar form is "older"; often Veytharisch preserves the edge while Veyrathi preserves the core.
