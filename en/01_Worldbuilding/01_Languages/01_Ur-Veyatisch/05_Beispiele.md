# Ur-Veyatic - examples

## Note on style effect
- Unmarked narrative speech can show early verb.
- Older table, council and oath languages often prefer the object or frame before the verb.
- The examples deliberately keep the language old and stubborn, not modern-national.

1. `Nu veyar.`
   I speak.

2. `Aer thalar draes.`
   The council is strong.

3. `Aer drun na nau torim.`
   My house is open.

4. `Nora aur kevar ta nela navar.`
   The Enor gives the Enel that contract.

5. `Aur toren nu seyaran.`
   I saw the gate.

6. `Vena se noi veyar, nu kai sherrar.`
   If the person doesn't speak, I would ask.

7. `Sena sha nu ta verun talar.`
   Today I will go to the port.

8. `Aer kevar ya nora werar zorin isar.`
   The contract that the Enor writes is valid.

9. `Zeya aur veyarat; draes aer enari.`
   That speech is new; the people are strong.

10. `Aur feyan aer enari jorar.`
    That sea binds the people.
