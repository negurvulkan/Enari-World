# Veyrathi - Enu section easy with Saithan glosses

## Purpose

This file provides a **light layman's version** of the Enu section in Veyrathi. The running text uses Veyrathi terms that are as simple as possible or are already well-known. The Saithan specialist forms are immediately followed in brackets.

Example principle:
- `pethglaren (mauthglarthim)` = Veyrathi suitable for everyday use plus Saithan technical term

## Reading rule
- First comes the simple Veyrathi form.
- The Saithan equivalent follows in brackets.
- If there is no fixed, simple single word, a short Veyrathi paraphrase precedes the Saithan form.

## Translation

### Haren va Ravath

Ar haren-jorath (harenthskor) enu'ren jora ar pethglaren (mauthglarthim), ar pethbulen (mauthdraskthrum), va ar ravath. Ar haren-glar ar demath-kor (thaelglarth) niva se. Ar peth-haren-har (mauthharenthum) fera ravath draz. Se fera ar thavar-umsor nari va ar darun-dirath len. Dar ar darun-varath, or haren draz hena va niva se dari.

### Outside ar Ushar-Janorath

Ar ushar-janorath (murnvargthrumskor) enu'ren tura ar fora-nukar (nukarthvenkskorn). Ar sivar-kulfir ve (nukarthvenkselkskelthim) tura oran. Ar fora-pethglaren (ärvmauthglarthim) tura oran. Se heva ar sivor shyul (mauthglarthmnaurim) ar sarel shi. Ar ushar-rer-opar ve (duvarmurndrethmnaurim) tura oran. Se jora ar usharbulen (murndraskthrum). Ar mur-opar (skarnmnaurbrimin) tura naror.

### Inside a Bulen

Ar pethbulen (mauthdraskthrum) fera ar draz bulen ar ushar-janorath (murnvargthrumskor). Se bava ar pethsuren (mauthstaursaiv). Ar pethsuren heva savar, korenlar, mirisar, yaran va relen. Haren or ar haren-glar ar demath-kor (thaelglarth) va or enu-janorath (särnvargthrumim) niva ar pethnava. Ar no-zyra-jan (harenthneskvailketh no tura). Se hena vath varath va ravath.

### Haurn va Ravath

Oran enu hena draz va haurn draz (haurnbrimselthum), ar pethglaren (mauthglarthim) yava draz. Sena enu heva ve varath ar janorath. Sena, ar pethsuren (mauthstaursaiv) bava ar jankap va nava thavar ar kylar oranges. Oranen, ar lirun or ar pethglaren (mauthglarthsaiv) nava pethan ar jankap-kylar va jora ar ravath.

### Pethsuren

Ar pethsuren (mauthstaursaiv) fera ar lirun draz. Se heva savar vuni, korenlar vuni, mirisar len, va yaran va relen. Se draz, silen-thunen, va len suren. Ena ar zeya haurn enari, se hena vuni va bava ar jankap ar yaran ärv. Senai, ar janakor hura se vuni va heva se, ena or jannavath hena.

### Oranges ar Jankap

Oranen ar jankap hena, enu niva ar ravath jorin. Sena ar pethglaren (mauthglarthim) niva ar lirun or ar pethglaren (mauthglarthsaiv) va ar zeya-kylar hena ar pethan savir. Ar ravath oranges fera draz jorin.

## German translation

### Hormones and care

The enu's hormonal network is closely linked to the mammary glands, the nutritive bladder and nursing. A central hormone gland in the inner control center regulates these processes. The care hormone complex makes the Enu more caring. The energy turnover remains calm and the tendency to argue becomes smaller. However, strong hormones can change this again in conflict or military service.

### External shape

The Enu urogenital complex is located in the anterior pelvic area. There are two pubic skin folds. The external nutritional glands are also located there. They have nine pores in three transverse rows. There are two urethral openings next to it. These lead to the urinary bladder. The excretory opening is further back on the back.### Inner bubble

Inside lies the nutritive bladder as a large organ of the urogenital complex. The nutritive matrix fluid is formed there. This fluid contains lipids, proteins, polysaccharides, water and electrolytes. Hormones from the central endocrine gland and the reproductive organs control secretion release. There is no fixed cycle. The levy follows the needs of reproduction and care.

### Growth and subsequent care

As enu grow larger and mature, their mammary glands also grow. They then take on two tasks in the reproductive system. The matrix fluid first prepares the spawning capsule and provides early energy to the young life. Later, the mammary gland releases a nourishing fluid that nourishes and supports the hatched child.

### The matrix fluid

The nutritive matrix liquid is a dense liquid. It contains many lipids and proteins, slightly fewer polysaccharides as well as water and electrolytes. It is thick, milky brown and has only a slight smell. In ancient times it was produced in large quantities and helped to protect spawning pods in open water bodies. Today, breeding centers continue to collect them and hold them until other reproductive contributions come along.

### After hatching

After hatching, enu look after the young animals together. Then the mammary glands release their nourishing fluid and the young child can easily absorb nutrition from it. This later care is also strongly organized communally.
