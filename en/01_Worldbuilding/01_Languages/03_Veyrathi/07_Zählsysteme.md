# Veyrathian payment system (V1.1)

This note describes the number system of the **Veyrathi language** as a leading culture standard: a **cultural quinar layer** (thinking in 5/25 bundles) plus a **decimal standardized professional layer** for administration, commerce and science. In technology/IT, work is done in binary internally, but is usually expressed in block-oriented representation (e.g. “hex-like”) and standardized in decimal.

---

## 1. Overview

Veyrathi has three registers that coexist:

- **Everyday life & culture (Quinar shaped):** Bundle numbers feel “round”; 25 is the central unit.
- **Standard language (decimal):** Standard terms for large powers of 10 (forms, statistics, science).
- **Technology/IT:** binary logic; external communication in decimal, internal abbreviations in block representation.

The system is built in such a way that it remains **learnable**: short basic words, clear bundles, simple addition connections.

---

## 2. Basic numbers (1–9)

### 2.1 Old roots (very stable)
| number | word |
|---:|---|
| 1 | **na** |
| 2 | **ve** |
| 3 | **shi** |
| 4 | **tho** |
| 5 | **kyu** |

### 2.2 Later Proper Nouns (6–9)
These are not “5+1” constructs, but independent number words that emerged later.

| number | word |
|---:|---|
| 6 | **nare** |
| 7 | **veshi** |
| 8 | **thona** |
| 9 | **shyul** |

6–9 are historically secondary proper nouns. Their current form is sometimes apparently transparent (e.g. veshi, thona), but is not understood synchronously as a composition.
---

## 3. Bundle numbers (multiples of 5)

Historically, these appear as “n × five” and are still recognizable in the standard spelling, but are often blurred.

| number | Morphology | Standard spelling | typical form of speech |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 10 | 2×5 | **ve-kyu** | **veyu** (optional: **ve’yu**) |
| 15 | 3×5 | **shi-kyu** | **shyuu** / **shyu** |
| 20 | 4×5 | **tho-kyu** | **thoyu** |

---

## 4. Additions (the binder **-ri-**)

Addition is expressed with **-ri-**:

- **X + Y = X-ri-Y**

Examples:
- 11 = **veyu-ri-na**
- 12 = **veyu-ri-ve**
- 14 = **veyu-ri-tho**
- 19 = **shyuu-ri-tho**
- 24 = **thoyu-ri-tho**

Multiple additions are allowed (read left to right):
- 49 = **kyu'u-ri-thoyu-ri-tho** (= 25 + 20 + 4)

---

## 5. Glottal stop ’ (pronunciation and spelling)

### 5.1 Function
In Veyrathi, the glottal stop **’** is primarily a **soft separating signal between vowels** (hiatus separator), especially at **bundle boundaries**.

- In **formal speech** ’ is more audible (clarity, counting announcements, teaching, administration).
- In **quick colloquial language** ’ can be weakened or omitted provided no ambiguity arises.

### 5.2 Orthography rule
The apostrophe **’** is **only used** when it is important for **clarity/distinction** (typical for numbers and bundles).

Examples:
- 25: **kyu’u** (recommended with ’)
- 10: **veyu** is default; **ve’yu** is permissible if you want to emphasize the bundle boundary.

---

## 6. The Central Bundle: 25 (**kyu’u**)

### 6.1 Definition
25 is “five fives”:
- 25 = **kyu-kyu** → **kyu’u**

The **spelling** may remain transparent (**kyu-kyu**), the **pronunciation** marks the bundle limit:
- **kyu’u** ≈ [kyuʔu](clearer in formal speech, softer in fast speech)

### 6.2 Numbers 25–49 (25 + remainder)
- 26 = **kyu’u-ri-na**
- 30 = **kyu’u-ri-kyu**
- 35 = **kyu’u-ri-shyuu**
- 40 = **kyu’u-ri-thoyu**
- 49 = **kyu'u-ri-thoyu-ri-tho**

---

## 7. Round multiplications by 25 (50 / 75 / 100)

Multiplication is used in everyday life primarily for **round** bundles:

- 2×X = **ve-X**
- 3×X = **shi-X**
- 4×X = **tho-X**
- 5×X = **kyu-X** (rarely used as X is often already a bundle)

Examples (based on 25):
- 50 = 2×25 = **ve-kyu’u** → **vekyu’u**
- 75 = 3×25 = **shi-kyu’u** → **shikyu’u** (colloquially often: **shyu’u**)
- 100 = 4×25 = **tho-kyu’u** → **thokyu’u** (colloquially often: **thoyu’u**)

Note on spelling:
- The **’** usually remains with **kyu’u** (clarity of numbers).
- It can be omitted in continuous text if it is clear (e.g. in dialogs).

---

## 8. Large cultural bundles (for warehouse/logistics)These words are intentionally **short** because they become common in accounting and commodity flows. They are derived from the 25 logic, but do not sound “earthly”.

| Value | Meaning | word |
|---:|---|---|
| 25² = 625 | Large bundle | **kyura** |
| 25³ = 15,625 | high bundle | **kyuran** |
| 25⁴ = 390,625 | Upper bundle | **kyurath** |
| 25⁵ = 9,765,625 | Giant bundle | **kyurazh** |

Typical use:
- Until **kyura**, people often thought of things in a “bundled” way.
- From **kyuran/kyurath** you quickly switch to rounding or the decimal norm in everyday life.

---

## 9. “Circa” and round information (realism rule)

Nobody says complicated exact tenders in bundles if it is of no use.

There is an approximation word for this:
- **sha** = “about / around and at”

Examples:
- “about a million” = **sha maren**
- “a good million” (cultural, rough) = **sha tho-kyurath**
- “about a thousand” (cultural) = **sha kyura** or **sha ve-kyura** (depending on region/precision)

---

## 10. Decimal Norm (Administration/Commerce/Science)

Veyrathi as a leading culture spreads a decimal technical language that dominates in forms, statistics and science.

| potency | Value | standard word |
|---:|---:|---|
| 10³ | 1,000 | **sen** |
| 10⁶ | 1,000,000 | **maren** |
| 10⁹ | 1,000,000,000 | **zhuren** |
| 10¹² | 1,000,000,000,000 | **tharen** |

Typical sentences:
- “It costs about a million.” → **sha maren**
- “Official: 1,000 pieces.” → **sen** (in documents), often spoken also **sen**.

---

## 11. Technology/IT (short note)

- Internal: **binary** (digital logic).
- Representation/speaking among experts: often **block/hex-like** (short groups instead of long decimals).
- To the outside: **Decimal norm** (sen/maren/…) or digits.

## 13. Note on translation into other Enari languages

Veyrathi serves as a reference. Adopt other languages:
- the **structure** (5/25 bundle + decimal standard layer),
- but change the **sound form** (sound laws, slurs, loan words),
- and vary the **register limits** (how early you switch to decimal norm).

Recommended workflow:
1. Keep Veyrathi canon stable (this file).
2. Define 5-8 sound rules per language (e.g. ky→ch, th→t, y→i, stop is omitted).
3. Derive number words systematically (no new “arbitrary” words necessary, except for dialect exceptions).
