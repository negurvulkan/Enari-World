# Veyrathi - Lexicon

## Lexical guidelines

- In the lexicon, verbs have the infinitive form `-a`.
- Strains should be usable for derivation with `-th` and `-or`.
- Unnecessary homonymy is avoided; necessary homonymy is marked.
- Natural synonymy is desirable when register, style or precision remain clearly distinguishable.
- Generic terms should remain actively used; If possible, new hyponyms are attached to existing hyperonyms.
- Role concepts remain gender-neutral by default; Pronouns carry Enari gender.
- New lexemes favor one or two syllables, open vowel leading and native consonant sequences instead of transparent earthly leanings.
- Specialist lexis is preferably built up from existing roots, derivation and composition.
- Numerals follow the canon from Chapter 07.

## Controlled homonymy

- Permitted: `ar` (article/demonstrative), `is` (pronoun/intensifier), `sen` (majority of people/number word/technical root), `vel` (adjective/dialogue signal)
- Marked but productively controlled: `kir` (free root in `kira` vs. bound prefix), `lir` (free root in `lira` vs. bound layer morpheme)
- To avoid: new lexemes as `no`, `sa`, `han`, `kei`, `ka`

## Data format (mandatory)

- Verbs: `| Veyrathi | IPA | Deutsch | Stamm |`
- Noun: `| Veyrathi | IPA | Deutsch | Plural |`
- Adjectives/adverbs/numerals/function words: `| Veyrathi | IPA | Wortart | Deutsch |`
- Proper names (myth): `| Form | IPA | Typ | Bedeutung im Mythos |`
- The IPA column reflects the broad teaching and standard pronunciation with word accent; Everyday language allophones are not specifically noted here.
- Compounds remain permitted as separate lexemes if they have a fixed meaning.

## Semantic depth relations (binding)

### Synonym families

- `java` / `bazora`: both "clarify, explain"; `java` sounds close to everyday life and direct, `bazora` systematic, didactic or formal.
- `rora` / `vaekeva`: both "negotiate"; `rora` has a market and conversational effect, `vaekeva` has an institutional or procedural effect.
- `sula` / `synzora`: both "agree"; `sula` is short, spontaneous and interactive, `synzora` is explicit and suitable for protocols.
- `dera` / `kirzora`: both "contradict"; `dera` is more everyday, `kirzora` more analytical or formal.
- `pora` / `retmyra`: both "remember"; `pora` is more personal and episodic, `retmyra` is knowledge-oriented and more systemic.
- `dena` / `vektala`: both "break"; `dena` is the living old form of everyday travel, `vektala` the transparent, system-oriented form of movement.
- `peta` / `dora`: both "buy"; `peta` names the specific act of acquisition, `dora` refers to the trading situation, market logic or negotiated procurement.
- `vel` / `zorin` / `savir`: can often be translated similarly in German; `vel` emphasizes clarity, `zorin` correctness or appropriateness, `savir` goodness and quality.

### Hyperonymic networks

- `nimen` is the neutral generic term for clothing; These include `kamen`, `valen`, `garen`, `pelen`, `moren`, `telar`.
- `lirun` is the generic term for liquids; These include `yaran`, `ushar`, `pethlirun`, `bindbul`, `pethsuren`.
- `rathen` is the generic term for narrative and report texts; These include `sarin-rathen`, `thalor-rathen`, `kethar-rathen`, `rathen-veyrath`.
- `kovath` is the generic term for building and large structures; These include `drun`, `sunel`, `vedun`, `rimen`, `moran-drun`.
- `kavar` is the generic term for paths and routes; These include `sela-kavar`, `mora-kavar`, `vethor`, `melyth-kavar`, `rakan-kavar`.
- `kevar` is the generic term for binding agreement and claim texts; These include `kethar-kevar`, `thalor-kevar`, `zanen-kevar`, `veylen-kevar`.

### Homonymy and resolution- `ar`: free article/demonstrative word; additionally bound prefix `ar-` with resultant value. Free vs. bound position resolves the ambiguity.
- `is`: Personal pronoun for `enis` in subject/object position versus intensifier directly before adjective or adverb.
- `sen`: neutral majority, number word `1000` and technical root in forms like `senvor`; Reference field and composition separate the readings.
- `vel`: Adjective “clear” versus dialogue signal “understood, good”; Prosody and sentence roll loosen the form.
- `kir`: free verb root in `kira` "cook" versus bound prefix `kir-` "separate/disassemble"; the fully developed word form decides.
- `lir`: free verb root in `lira` "read" versus bound histology morpheme `-lir` "layer"; the morphological position decides.

## Quick compatibility notes

- Verbs on `-a` can be directly combined with tense suffixes (`-n`, `-ra`).
- New connectors remain separate from open lexeme classes.

## Derivative application in the lexicon

- Verb nominalization follows productive `Stamm + -ath` (orthography from `-th` with epenthetic `a` after consonant stem).
- Agent formation follows productive `Stamm + -or`.
- Noun -> adjective follows productively `Nomen + -i` (e.g. `kevar -> kevari`, `vethan -> vethani`, `talen -> taleni`).
- Compounds remain right-headed and are considered separate lexemes if they have a fixed technical meaning.
