# Veyrathi - Place names

## Purpose

This file specifies a productive system for Veyrathian place names. She should:
- Systematize the names of landscapes and bodies of water
- Make settlement and infrastructure names plausible
- make historical deep layers from Ur-Veyatic and Old-Veyata usable
- make western or Veytharian-influenced variants predictable

## Initial situation

There is already enough material for toponymy:
- right thinking in the composition
- a stable sound profile for new lexemes
- many local and landscape roots
- historical precursors for older layers of names
- Western phonetic equivalents for contact and boundary forms

Toponyms therefore do not have to be invented freely, but can be built systematically from the existing vocabulary.

## Basic principles

### 1. Right-headedness

As in the general lexicon, the last link is the core:
- `Norel-talen` = City on the river
- `Meral-vilar` = mountain village
- `Feyan-verun` = Seaport

### 2. Three main layers

Place names usually come from three layers:

- Hereditary toponyms:
  old, often monosyllable or two-syllable names that come directly from landscape words or ancient roots
  Examples: `Meral`, `Senel`, `Verun`

- descriptive compounds:
  transparent, modern names based on location type, function or location
  Examples: `Kethar-talen`, `Norel-miral`, `Ravath-vilar`

- historical or mythical names:
  older, less transparent names with Ur-Veyatic or Old-Veyatic depth or with reference to myth and council history
  Examples: `Avar-talen`, `Syra-norel`, `Thalar-karen`

### 3. Place names are semantically clearer than personal names

Veyrathi prefers high readability in place names:
- Location
- Function
- Landscape
- route
- institutional role

And less:
- pure dynastic logic
- bare patronymics
- opaque phonetic ornaments

### 4. Old forms can be harder

In the everyday lexicon, Veyrathi is very smooth. Older remains may be retained longer in toponyms:
- `-ar`, `-un`, `-el`, `-en` remain well in old place names
- old diphthongs or heavier word edges can remain fossilized
- this is especially true for rivers, coasts, mountains, harbors and old council places

## Historical layers

### Ur-Veyatic

Ur-veyatic toponyms are particularly useful for:
- Rivers
- Coasts
- Mountains
- old council and harbor towns
- cult or epic landscapes

Features:
- slightly heavier word edges
- clearer `r` and `n` finals
- occasionally archaic diphthong or full old stem formSuch names may be preserved directly in modern Veyrathi or may live on only slightly smoothed.

### Old Veyata

Alt-Veyata is the best source for:
- old market and council towns
- supra-regional trading locations
- historical Koine names
- fuller, but still easily legible local forms

Features:
- more open full shapes than in the West
- more old ending syllables than in the modern standard
- very suitable for older city and institution names

### Modern Veyrathi

Modern names prefer:
- transparent composition
- clear two or three syllables
- functional readability

This is the default layer for:
- new cities
- Quarters
- Transport locations
- modern port and station names

### Western and Veytharian influenced variants

In western contact zones these tendencies often apply:
- `ey` -> `ei`
- open ending syllables become scarcer
- `-ath` -> `-eth`
- some old cores are shortened:
  `kethar -> keth`, `toren -> tor`

This means that western parallel forms can emerge from a Veyrathian place name without having to invent a completely new name.

## Typical place name types

### 1. Direct inheritance toponyms

An old landscape word becomes a place name:
- `Meral`
- `Senel`
- `Verun`
- `Naar`

This is particularly suitable for:
- ancient mountains
- old ports
- Coastal strip
- Forest areas

### 2. Descriptive settlement names

Pattern:
- `[Landschaft] + talen`
- `[Landschaft] + vilar`
- `[Funktion] + talen`
- `[Qualitaet] + verun`

Examples:
- `Norel-talen`
- `Meral-vilar`
- `Kethar-talen`
- `Savir-verun`

### 3. Functional locations

Pattern:
- `[Rolle/Funktion] + [Platz/Bau/Route]`

Examples:
- `Thalor-karen`
- `Ravath-drun`
- `Vethan-jeren`
- `Sirath-vethor`

### 4. Mytho-historical names

Pattern:
- `[Mythosname] + [Toponymkopf]`
- `[archaischer Stamm] + [Toponymkopf]`

Examples:
- `Avar-talen`
- `Syra-norel`
- `Eran-meral`
- `Ilyr-selar`

### 5. Fossilized hybrid forms

Some names remain semi-transparent:
- old basic word remains
- new town header is added
- Western variant only shortens a part later

Examples:
- `Kethar-talen` -> western `Keth-talen`
- `Toren-karen` -> western `Tor-karen`
- `Sirath-meral` -> western `Sireth-meral`

### 6. Places of Origin and Institution

Pattern:
- `[Funktion] + janakor`
- `[Zustaendigkeit] + orven`
- `[Netz/Ort] + niva`

Examples:
- `Ravath-janakor`
- `Vethan-orven`
- `Talen-niva`

The specialized elaboration for children's house and `janakor` names follows in the additional module `18_Kinderhausnamen.md`.

## Naming motifs

Toponyms become most plausible in Veyrathi when they bear one of these motifs:
- Landscape: river, forest, mountain, valley, field
- Function: market, council, port, gate, bridge
- Location: far, near, above, below, inside, outside
- social origin and institution: children's home, archive, guard, workshop, control center
- History: founding, binding, judgment, protection, departure
- Myth: Avar, Eran, Syra, Ilyr and cosmological old words

Less typical are:
- long patronymic rows
- pure ruler eponyms with no reference to function or landscape
- openly Saithan everyday place names

## Saithan influence

Saithanic can affect place names, but not as a default layer for normal settlements. It is particularly plausible for:
- Observatories
- Central archives
- old teaching houses
- Codex and chancellery districts
- sacred or learned complexes

Normal cities, villages, rivers and ports generally remain built in Veyat style.

## Quick rules for new place names

1. First determine the location type:
   River, bank, mountain, forest, village, city, place of residence, farm, work place, harbor, square, bridge, path, gate, border, pass, control center.

2. Then select the core:
   `norel`, `lavin`, `noren`, `meral`, `doren`, `senel`, `selun`, `selar`, `vilar`, `talen`, `lonath`, `talem`, `verun`, `drun`, `rimen`, `garun`, `orven`, `janakor`, `karen`, `miral`, `vethor`, `muren`, `jeren`, `niva`.

3. Add a determiner:
   Landscape, function, location, history or myth.

4. Check readability:
   The name in Veyrathi should remain fluid and not appear overloaded.

5. Decide the shift:
   Hereditary toponym, Old Veyata full form, modern compound or western contact form.
